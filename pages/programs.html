What We Do

Our Programs

A comprehensive approach from prevention to restoration, addressing the full spectrum of anti-trafficking work.

The Alabaster Jar implements a comprehensive operational framework spanning prevention, rescue, rehabilitation, and restoration. Our programs are designed to address every stage of a survivor's journey‚Äîfrom the moment of rescue through complete reintegration into society as thriving, self-sufficient individuals.

Rescue & Recovery Restoration Prevention Empowerment
Program 1

üÜò Rescue & Recovery Operations

Goal: Partner with national and international stakeholders to identify, rescue, and protect victims of human trafficking through coordinated, trauma-informed interventions.

Partnership Development

  • Develop and maintain MOUs with law enforcement, immigration, border patrol, and cybercrime units
  • Coordinate with U.S. agencies including FBI Human Trafficking Units and DHS
  • Build relationships with Royal Thai Police and Department of Special Investigation
  • Coordinate with Mexican and Colombian authorities as operations expand

Field Operations

  • Train and deploy on-the-ground teams for real-time rescue missions
  • Maintain standby rescue teams with 24/7 response capability
  • Provide specialized support during large-scale law enforcement operations
  • Deploy mobile response units for remote or underserved areas

Safe Housing Network

  • Establish and operate safe houses with international security standards
  • Partner with existing shelters in India, Colombia, and Mexico
  • Develop emergency placement protocols for immediate post-rescue housing
  • Create specialized housing for different populations (minors, men, families)

Rapid Recovery Protocol (72 Hours)

  • Immediate care kit including clothing, toiletries, and comfort items
  • Comprehensive medical examination and treatment
  • Psychological first aid from trained trauma counselors
  • Spiritual support, safety planning, and legal advocacy connection
Program 2

üíö Restoration & Rehabilitation Services

Goal: Provide trauma-informed care that addresses the physical, emotional, and spiritual needs of survivors through evidence-based, holistic programming.

Therapeutic Services

  • Individual counseling using EMDR, Cognitive Processing Therapy, and Somatic Experiencing
  • Group therapy for trauma bonding, complex PTSD, and healthy relationships
  • Art therapy, music therapy, and other expressive therapies
  • Faith-based counseling options for spiritual integration
  • Family therapy and reunification support when appropriate

Spiritual Care Programs

  • Spiritual retreats centered on healing and identity in Christ
  • Bible study groups and discipleship programs
  • Worship gatherings and prayer ministry
  • Spiritual mentorship pairing survivors with trained mentors
  • Interfaith accommodation for different faith backgrounds

Healthcare Services

  • Comprehensive physical health assessments and ongoing care
  • Dental and vision care services
  • Reproductive health services and education
  • Substance abuse treatment and recovery support
  • Psychiatric services and medication management

Transitional Housing Program

  • Phase 1: High-support residential with 24/7 staffing
  • Phase 2: Semi-independent living with regular check-ins
  • Phase 3: Independent housing with community connections
  • Aftercare support including alumni programs and crisis intervention
Program 3

üß† Mental Health & Prevention Programs

Goal: Address root causes of exploitation by transforming both survivors and potential perpetrators while educating communities to prevent trafficking.

Community Education & Awareness

  • School workshops on trafficking red flags and online safety
  • Church-based education programs
  • Community seminars on prevention
  • Professional training for healthcare workers, teachers, and social workers
  • Age-appropriate curriculum materials

Demand Reduction Programs

  • Men's recovery groups for addiction and compulsive behavior
  • Programs addressing toxic masculinity and healthy relationships
  • Clinical treatment partnerships
  • "John School" diversion programs
  • Public awareness campaigns

Church Mobilization

  • Train churches as safe spaces for conversations
  • Equip leaders for pastoral care
  • Develop prayer networks
  • Create volunteer pathways
  • Host awareness and fundraising events
Program 4

üìö Reskilling & Economic Empowerment

Goal: Equip survivors with the tools to become self-sufficient and contribute meaningfully to their communities through education, vocational training, and entrepreneurship support.

Vocational Training Centers

Programs offered include:

  • Handicrafts: Jewelry-making, sewing, embroidery, artisan crafts
  • Food Services: Baking, catering, barista training, certification
  • Cleaning Services: Residential, commercial, hospitality
  • Administrative Support: Virtual assistance, bookkeeping, event planning
  • Beauty & Wellness: Cosmetology, nail technology, massage therapy
  • Healthcare Support: CNA, home health aide, medical admin

Education & Digital Literacy

  • Basic education and adult schooling for those denied formal education
  • GED and high school equivalency preparation
  • Computer literacy: typing, Microsoft Office, email, online safety
  • English language learning (ESL/EFL)
  • Pathways to higher education through scholarships

Digital Economy Opportunities

  • Remote customer service positions
  • Freelance graphic design (Canva, Adobe Express)
  • Virtual assistance and administrative support
  • Content moderation and data entry
  • Social media management for local businesses
  • E-commerce support (Etsy, Shopify)
  • Transcription, translation, freelance writing

Financial & Employment Empowerment

  • Financial literacy classes: budgeting, saving, planning
  • Assistance opening bank accounts and establishing credit
  • Matched savings programs (Individual Development Accounts)
  • Resume development and interview preparation
  • Mentorship and job placement programs
  • Microenterprise and cooperative development
